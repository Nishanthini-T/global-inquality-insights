High Inequality Alert

South Africa has one of the highest Gini coefficients globally at 63.0, indicating severe income inequality. The top 10% of the population controls approximately 65% of national wealth, while the bottom 50% owns less than 5%.

Trend Analysis

Despite government intervention programs, inequality has remained persistently high over the past decade. The poverty rate shows a slight decline from 21.2% in 2015 to 18.9% in 2025, but progress is slower than regional averages.

Key Drivers

Major factors contributing to inequality include: historical legacy of apartheid, skills mismatch in the labor market, high unemployment rate (32.5%), and unequal access to quality education and healthcare services.

Predictive Outlook

Based on current trends and AI modeling, the Gini index is projected to decrease marginally to 61.5 by 2030 if targeted interventions in education, job creation, and social protection are implemented effectively.
